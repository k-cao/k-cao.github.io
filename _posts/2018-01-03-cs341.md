CS 341 Algorithms is noted as a particularly useful course; after all, algorithms is the very core of computer science. Not to mention that this class has proven itself useful in job interviews and the workplace.

However, it is also a difficult course. Thus, I started pre-viewing previous terms' lecture notes as preparation. The following material is a compilation of my notes. None of the ideas or material presented is mine, they are merely edits and conglomerations of the lectures I have seen.

The sources I have used are notes from professors [Lap Chi Lau](https://cs.uwaterloo.ca/~lapchi/cs341/notes.html), Anna Lubiw, and Semih Salihoglu.

I hope this will be of help to fellow CS and SE students as well as for anyone interested in algorithms.

These notes will be edited and added over the course of this term.
